# Ekspansja: Ekspansja i struktura państwa

**Okres:** 1025 - 1138
**Epoka:** Piastowie

## Przegląd okresu

Okres od koronacji Bolesława Chrobrego (1025) do śmierci Bolesława Krzywoustego (1138) marks the final phase of early Piast monarchy and the transition toward feudal fragmentation. After Chrobry's death, his son Mieszko II faced a coalition of German, Rus, and Czech forces that led to temporary loss of royal title and territorial concessions. The pagan revolt of 1037-1038 and Czech invasion under Brzetyslav devastated Great Poland, destroying key centers like Gniezno and Poznań.

Kazimierz Odnowiciel (1039-1058) gradually restored state structures with imperial and Rus assistance, shifting the political center to less-affected Kraków. He reintegrated Mazovia (1047) and Silesia (1050), though losing royal title status. His successors—Bolesław Śmiały (1058-1079) and Władysław Herman (1079-1102)—alternated between assertive policies and dependence on magnates, with the latter losing control over Pomerania.

Bolesław Krzywousty (1102-1138) reconquered Pomerania (1116-1122), Christianized the region through Otto of Bamberg's missions, and defended independence against Emperor Henryk V (1109). His testament of 1138 introduced the principle of seniorate, attempting to maintain unity while granting hereditary duchies to sons, inadvertently initiating Poland's feudal fragmentation that would last nearly two centuries.
